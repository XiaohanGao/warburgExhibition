A Confectionery Map in 1815

19th century sees great development in confectionery industry thanks to the mechanisation. There emerged more manufacturers, more pastry shops, more varieties of confectionery and more consumers who could afford the sweet treats. 

The interactive map below shows pastry and confectionery shops from an anually living guide handbook "Calendar of Good Living 1815". "Calendar of Good Living 1815" is a directory to the traverns, coffee-houses, inns, eating-houses and other places of alimentary resort in the British Metropolis and its Environs.

Most of the shops in the 1815 handbook do not exist in today's London map. However, it is interesting to know the 200 years ago Londoner's life and to figure out how much our modern life could interact with the past by geo locations through digital approaches.
